VERIFICATION OF THE NWA CALIBRATION

In order to verify the previously performed NWA calibration, and its calibration plane shift down to the GSG probe tips, it is highly recommended to
  • re-measure the calibration standards,
  • to model them corresponding to the CalKit definitions,
  • and to compare the resulting model parameter values with the original CalKit definitions.
This allows to detect
  • issues with errors made when entering the CalKit data into the NWA,
  • issues with the mechanical performance of the probes and the cables,
  • or issues with the NWA performance itself.



Depicted above is an example of a successful calibration verification for GSG probes, verified with re-measured and modeled OPEN and SHORT ISS Cal.Standards.
On the top, the we have the OPEN verification, and on the bottom, the SHORT verification.
In both cases, the S-parameters of the re-measured Cal.Standards are converted into their corresponding CalKit definitions (the OPEN S-parameters into a capacitance at each port, and the SHORT into an inductance at each port).
The obtained values -as a function of frequency- represent a very good match to the original CalKit definitions.

The verification results of the LOAD and the THRU are shown in the next slide.



The calibration verification is now applied to the re-measured LOAD and the THRU on the ISS Cal.Substrate. Like before, a very good agreement of the converted S-parameters to the original CalKit specifications is achieved.

Conclusions:
Only if all the four, re-measured Calibration Standards exhibit an excellent fit compared to the Spice simulations of their electric property definitions, we can assume a correct calibration of the NWA.
An Example of a Bad GSG Calibration Verification Result



Shown above is an example of a not so successful GSG probes cal. verification

Comments on the re-measured OPEN CalKit Standard:
the re-measured ISS OPEN correctly turns backwards, as given by the OPEN Cal.Standard definition of a negative capacitance of C0 = -9.7fF.
However, the magnitudes of S11 and also S22 increase, for incrementing frequency, to values above 1, what corresponds to a negative resistance, what is physically incorrect.
-> the calibration is invalid and has to be repeated.

Comments on the re-measured SHORT CalKit Standard:
here, the re-measured Port1 resistance is different from the one of Port2, and the Port2 resistance is even negative !
Additionally, the Port1 and Port2 inductances are different (Phase of S11 and S22):
at Port1, the inductance is ~20% too big (red trace), and at Port2 (blue trace), it is ~50% too small compared to the expected, simulated CalKit definition of 4.8pH (green curve).
-> the calibration is invalid and has to be repeated.
If the problem persists, there might be a problem with the mechanics of the GSG probes.

Comments on the re-measured THRU CalKit Standard:



The contact problem of the GSG probes, already detected with the SHORT verification before, is showing up again also for the THRU re-measurement.
  • The phase delay of the THRU is first negative (low freq.) and then turns positive for higher frequencies, as expected.
  • In addition, there magnitude of both, S21 and S12, is considerably lower than the target value of '1'.
    • The calibration is invalid. A mechanical problem with the GSG probe tip contacts is assumed.
Weighting the Cal. Verification Measurements:

The importance weighting, i.e. the order of cal. verification challenges, is
  • SHORT (most challenging),
  • OPEN,
  • THRU,
  • LOAD (least challenging).
