Interpreting the S11 and S22 Parameters




Interpreting S11 and S22 by Applying a Smith Chart What makes Sxx-Parameters especially interesting for modeling, is that S11 and S22 can be interpreted as complex input or output impedance of the Two-Port (don't forget: including the termination at the opposite side of the Two-Port with Z0 !!). That's why they are usually plotted in a Smith chart.


Demo Movie: See AN-95-1A.mp4

The Smith chart is a transformation of the Impedance Plane Z into the Reflection S-Parameter Sxx, following:

    with the system's reference impedance Z0, usually Z0 = 50 Ohm.
This means that the right half of the complex impedance plane Z is transformed into the area of a circle in the Sxx-domain, with radius '1' .
In order to get more familiar with interpreting the Smith chart, a green square is indicated above, in the complex impedance plane 'Z', with the corners 1 (0/0) Ohm, corner 2 (50/0) Ohm, corner 3 (50/j50) Ohm and corner 4 (0/j50) Ohm, and its equivalent shape in the Smith Chart with Z0=50 Ohm is drawn too.
Watch the angel-preserving property of this transform (rectangles stay rectangles close to their origins). Also watch how the points 1 and 4, on the positive imaginary axis of the Z plane, are transformed into the Smith Chart, and where (50/j50) Ohm (point 3) is located in the Smith Chart.
Also verify that the center of the Smith Chart represents Z0 (point 2).

NOTES:
  • The magnitudes of S11 and S22 are always ≤1, i.e. inside the Smith Chart.
    • Measurement results with Sxx outside the Smith Chart would mean measuring a power plant (negative resistance !). Consider to re-calibrate your NWA.
  • Remember that included in S-Parameters is the termination of the device at the opposite side of the two-port, by Z0 !!
The Different Regions of a Smith Chart


From the value of Sxx Parameters in the Smith Chart, we can calculate the value of the input or output impedance of the device as:
    Z = Z0 * (1+Sxx) / (1-Sxx)

In details:
  • resistors are frequency-independent. Therefore, their reflection coefficient is represented by a dot, for all frequencies, on the x-axis, corresponding to their resistance value.
    A resistor with 0 Ohm, connected between one Port and Ground, corresponds to Sxx = -1 (to the left of the Smith Chart), one with 50 Ohm to Sxx = 0 lies in the center, and one with infinite Ohm to Sxx = +1 on the right of the Smith Chart (green trace).
  • a capacitor between Port1 and Ground behaves like an OPEN for freq → 0Hz: Sxx = +1,
    and like a SHORT for freq → ∞: Sxx = -1
    • With increasing frequency, the trace of a capacitor is represented by a half-circle, turning clockwise with frequency (blue trace).
  • finally, an inductor between Port1 and Ground behaves like a SHORT for freq → 0Hz: Sxx = -1, and like an OPEN for freq → ∞: Sxx = +1
    With increasing frequency, the trace of an inductor is represented by a half-circle, turning clockwise with frequency (red trace).


As a general rule, note that the Sxx curves always turn to the right with increasing frequency. If not, there is either a NWA calibration problem, a measurement problem, or a de-embedding problem.
Interpreting the S21 and S12 Parameters


Interpretation of the S21/S12 Real Part Value: Device Transmission Properties


As a rule of thumb: you may interpret the transfer Sxy Parameter values like percent of power transmission between the two ports.
Interpreting S12 and S21 by Applying a Polar Plot


  • resistors are frequency-independent. Therefore, their transmission coefficient is represented by a dot, for all frequencies (green trace). A resistor with 0 Ohm, connected between Port1 and Port2, corresponds to Sxy = +1 (to the right of the Polar Plot), and one with infinite Ohm is at Sxy = 0, in the center of the Polar Plot (no transmission).
  • a capacitor between Port1 and Port2 behaves like an OPEN for freq →0Hz: S12=S21=0 (no transmission), and like a SHORT for freq → ∞: S12=S21=1 (full power transmission). With increasing frequency, the trace of a capacitor is represented by a half-circle, turning clockwise with frequency (blue trace).
  • finally, an inductor between Port1 and Port2 behaves like a SHORT for freq → 0Hz: S12=S21=1, and like an OPEN for freq → ∞: S12=S21=0. With increasing frequency, the trace of an inductor is represented by a half-circle, turning clockwise with frequency (red trace).

    • S-Parameter Interpretation in a Nut Shell
  • Sxx Parameters, displayed in a Smith Chart, can easily be interpreted as Input or Output Impedance of the Two-Port.
  • Sxy Parameters, displayed in a Polar Plot, can easily be interpreted as Transmitted Power between the ports.
    • - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -
  • The magnitude of Sxx Parameters is always <1. Otherwise, it would be a negative resistance, and outside of the Smith Chart !
  • Sxy Parameters for amplifying components have a magnitude >1, (provided the component can amplify in a 50 Ohm system).
  • With increasing frequency, from 0 -> infinite, all S-parameter curves turn clock-wise !
    • - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -
  • The start of the trace at fmin, extended down to freq=0, leads to the Spice schematic for DC
  • and the end, at fmax, extended up to infinite frequency, leads to the Spice schematic for AC.
  • For simple Spice schematics, the trace, in between fmin and fmax, is by first order a half-circle of the corresponding Spice component.
