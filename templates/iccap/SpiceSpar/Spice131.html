Spice Interpretation of S-Parameters

INTERPRETING S-PARAMETERS BY SPICE SCHEMATICS

by Franz Sischka, www.SisConsult.de
S-Parameter Plots can directly be interpreted with respect to Spice schematics. This enables a direct inspection and quality assessment of a measurement result, just after it has been performed.

A very important pre-requisite, however, is verified S-Parameter measurements. Besides an accurate and verified network analyzer calibration (see the separate chapter), we also need accurate and verified de-embedding structures, on the same wafer as the device.



It is strongly recommended to have all three dummies (OPEN, SHORT and THRU) available on the wafer, and to develop Spice models for each of them.
With this modeling, we check:
  • if there are really no series components hidden in the OPEN dummy,
  • no parallel components hidden in the SHORT,
  • and if the THRU (as a substitute of the later DUT), and de-embedded from both, the OPEN and the SHORT, can be modeled by a simple, lossless delay line.

For details, see the separate chapter.

Once this is done, the DUT measurement itself is de-embedded, and its S-Parameter plots are inspected:
  • do the de-embedded trace turn clock-wise, also for high frequencies ?
  • or every de-embedding step, is there less phase ?
  • are the de-embedded traces physical, e.g. within the Smith Chart ?



In the example above, all de-embedded S-Parameter traces exhibit less phase, and represent the basic properties of an on-wafer capacitance: the Spice interpretation can be applied, as explained next.

Conclusions:
Inspecting S-Parameters with respect to an underlying Spice schematic
  • gives first-order results
  • works well for low frequencies
