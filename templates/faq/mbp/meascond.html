Use MeasCond to Put Similar Sweeps into Different Groups

MBP FAQ

Sometimes we have 2 measurement data files for the same device but with very similar bias sweeps, for example:
  • Ids_vds_high_vgs
  • Ids_vds_low_vgs
    • (Note: click on the files to download them.)

Where they are both Ids_vds_vgs sweeps for the same W/L/T=10/10/25 devices, just the Vgs values are different in two files.

By default, MBP parses in measurement data files by telling the device fingerprint, such as W/L/T, and biasing pattern, namely whether it’s Ids_vds_vgs, or Ids_vgs_vbs, etc. Here comes the problem, the “low_vgs” and “high_vgs” are not recognized by MBP! When we try to load the two files at the same time, only one got loaded in and another one gets overwritten.

The workaround is to introduce another identifier, which is a keyword called MeasCond , so that we would be able to load these two files into MBP at the same time, and see them under “ids_vds_high_vgs” and “ids_vds_low_vgs” folders on the GUI, as shown below.
