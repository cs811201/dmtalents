Understand System Noise Floor

ALFNA FAQ

ALFNA system’s LNA noise floor is stated as the following in the brochure, there are 3 voltage LNAs and 2 current LNAs, as follows, they are a major factor of the system’s overall noise floor; however, there are more to consider when we understand the validity of the measurement data.



In the RawData view of a measured 1/f curve, the software presents us the following additional info related to the measurement, as labeled on the graph, to help us understand the validity of the data.
    1. the DUT’s noise data, the red is the raw measured data, which has a central yellow curve that is post-processed data with smoothness.
    2. VAMP voltage LNA amplifier’s input noise.
    3. Thermal noise level of the RLoad.
    4. SMU noise, such as a HRSMU of B1500A.
    5. Roll-off frequency.


The system’s noise floor is considered as the combination of 2, 3 and 4.
    1. Any part of the DUT’s noise data that is lower than either one of the curves becomes invalid data;
    2. and, DUT’s noise data after roll-off frequency is also invalid.
